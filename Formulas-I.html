    Cost production:

    The cost is the economic expense that is carried out to buy or maintain a service or a product. The concept of production, on the other hand, refers to the action of producing (elaborating, manufacturing, originating). It is the set of expenses that are necessary to produce a service or a good.

    Production costs can be fixed costs (which remain stable in the face of changes in the production level) or variable costs (they change as the volume of production is altered).

    we have to establish that within the costs of production there are two other types clearly differentiated. We are referring to the following: - Direct costs, which are those that are linked and related to each item that occurs. Example of these would be the money that costs to buy the raw material with which it is created. - Indirect costs, which are those that do not directly affect that article. Examples of this type of production costs are the salaries of the workers in the administrative area of the company and the cost of renting the premises where it is located.


    Cost distribution:

    they are all the disbursements incurred by a producer to get their products, from their manufacturing plant to the place where they are acquired, thus constituting a fundamental component of the total cost to the producer and the price paid by consumers, therefore his analysis is very important in terms of business efficiency.

    There are four factors to consider in order to apply the distribution costs:

    1. The creation of demand: how consumers' interest in the product is aroused, includes, among other activities, advertising and sales promotion.

    2. Obtention of the order: includes the expenses inherent to the sales force.

    3. Product handling and delivery: includes transportation, storage and other logistics activities.

    4. Control of the sale: includes the investigation and opening of the credit, accounting routine for its registration, preparation of sales analysis, collection service and all other inherent functions until the sale is translated into money.

    This method has its origin in accounting by areas of responsibility, which avoids certain dangers of arbitrarily spilling the distribution costs between market segments, which can create some confusion, which can be avoided if the cost system is established of distribution, certain control factors are used, that is, the cost determined by geographical area. When analyzing the costs of distribution by order amounts, those costs that vary in proportion to the number of orders processed, provide a logical basis for application to obtain the profits generated by each order.
